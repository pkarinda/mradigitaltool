About the EAC
Mutual Recognition Portal

The East African Community (EAC) is committed to enabling the free movement of labor within its Partner States by harmonizing and mutually recognizing academic and professional qualifications. However, despite the existence of the Mutual Recognition Agreement (MRA) for engineers, professionals frequently encounter obstacles when trying to have their licenses acknowledged across borders. The primary issue is the absence of a systematic process for the exchange of information between the Competent Authorities (CAs) responsible for issuing and recognizing professional licenses.

This deficiency creates significant challenges and delays in the issuance of mutual recognition credentials, making it difficult for engineers to practice in different EAC Partner States. The EAC Secretariat's innovative digital tool aims to address these challenges by enhancing the recognition and mobility of professional engineers throughout the region. This initiative is a crucial step towards achieving greater regional integration and cooperation among the Partner States.


FAQ


The mutual recognition of professional qualifications is an agreement among the East African Community (EAC) Partner States that allows engineers who are licensed in one EAC country to have their qualifications recognized in another Partner State without needing to requalify. This process facilitates the free movement of skilled professionals across borders, enabling engineers to work in different EAC countries with ease. The mutual recognition agreement (MRA) ensures that engineers can offer their services throughout the region, contributing to regional integration, economic development, and access to a broader job market.

To apply for mutual recognition of qualifications in another EAC Partner State, an engineer must submit an application to the Competent Authority (CA) of the country where they wish to work. The application process typically involves providing necessary documentation, including proof of qualifications, professional experience, and current licensure from their home country. The respective CA will review the submission, validate the credentials, and either approve, reject, or request additional information. Once approved, the engineer will be granted the necessary credentials to practice in the new country under the mutual recognition framework.

To apply for mutual recognition of their qualifications in another EAC Partner State, an engineer must meet several prerequisites. These typically include holding a valid engineering license or certification from their home country, having the necessary academic and professional qualifications recognized by the Competent Authority (CA) in their home state, and demonstrating relevant professional experience. Additionally, engineers may be required to provide proof of continuous professional development and comply with any specific requirements or regulations set by the CA of the country where they seek recognition. Meeting these prerequisites ensures that the engineer is qualified to practice at a professional standard consistent with the expectations of the host country.
